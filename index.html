About the Event
What Is the Bean Pot Rally?

The Bean Pot Rally is an informal, community-driven gathering of Land Rover owners and enthusiasts from across New England. Held at the Sebasticook Millennium Green in Unity, Maine — the same stunning property that hosts the annual Winter Romp — the Bean Pot Rally offers a full weekend of guided trail rides, camaraderie, and a proper Maine bean pot supper.

Whether you drive a Series I, a classic Range Rover, or a brand-new Defender, you're welcome. Trails are rated Green, Blue, and Black so there's something for every skill level and every truck. Novice? No problem — experienced drivers are happy to help guide you down the trail. The event is non-competitive and generally non-damaging (though off-roading always carries risk — you're responsible for your rig).

The Bean Pot Rally is organized by the Massachusetts Rover Society (MARS), the Down East Land Rover Club (DELRC), Connecticut Rovers, and the Northeast Land Rover Club (NELRC), with support from Land Rover clubs and ANARC. A portion of proceeds will be donated to a local charity.

All Skill Levels

Green, Blue, and Black rated trails with experienced trail leaders.

All Land Rovers

Series, Defenders, Discos, Range Rovers, Freelanders — if it wears the green oval, it belongs here.

Camp or Hotel

Primitive and powered camping on-site, or stay at the Waterville Grand Hotel 15 minutes away.

Give Back

A portion of proceeds goes to a local charity. We believe in giving back to the communities that host us.

What to Expect
A Weekend in the Maine Woods

Guided Trail Rides

Green, Blue, and Black rated trails through woodland, ridgelines, and water crossings. All pre-run with experienced trail leaders.

Bean Pot Supper

The main event — traditional slow-baked Maine beans, brown bread, and red snapper hot dogs. Saturday evening under the pines.

Camping On-Site

Primitive camping in the powerline corridor and fields. RVs welcome near the barn. Nearby campgrounds and hotels too.

Recovery Demos

Recovery and first-aid demonstrations from ANARC stewards. Learn proper technique from the best.

Movie Night

Friday evening movie streamed on the Green — plus an optional night trail ride for the adventurous.

Vendors & Raffle

Land Rover dealers and aftermarket specialists on display. Raffle prizes, giveaways, and a community bonfire to close the night.

The Venue
Sebasticook Millennium Green

The Sebasticook Millennium Green is a private off-road property in Unity, Maine, featuring miles of woodland trails, open fields, ridgelines, and water crossings. The property has hosted the Winter Romp for years and offers terrain for every skill level — from gently rolling forest roads to rocky, technical climbs.

Amenities include a barn with running water, staging areas, and camping in the powerline corridor and surrounding fields. RVs welcome near the barn.

Camping

Primitive camping on-site at the powerline and staging area. RVs can park near the barn with running water. Porta-potties will be available. Greenwood Acres, a nearby campground, offers additional tent camping.

Hotel

The Waterville Grand Hotel (Best Western Plus) in Waterville is about 15 minutes away and has a block of rooms reserved for the event. Details to follow.

Be Prepared
What to Bring
▸ Your Land Rover — registered, insured, properly maintained
▸ A working CB or GMRS radio
▸ Front and rear recovery points
▸ Appropriate tires for the conditions
▸ Tow strap and shackles
▸ Food and water for the trail
▸ Warm layers and rain gear
▸ The right attitude
If your rig isn't properly prepared, you may be asked to ride along with another driver. For a full equipment list, check the Winter Romp's excellent guidance at winterromp.me.
